Chuck Norris: Legende

Chuck Norris is an American actor and martial artist who rose to fame in the 1970s and 1980s with his roles in a number of action films. He was born Carlos Ray Norris on March 10, 1940, in Ryan, Oklahoma, and grew up in Torrance, California. Norris began his martial arts training at the age of six, and by the time he was in his 20s, he had earned a black belt in Tang Soo Do, a Korean martial art. He later became the first Westerner to be promoted to an 8th degree black belt in Taekwondo.

In the late 1960s and early 1970s, Norris made a name for himself as a martial arts instructor, and he opened several karate schools in the United States. He also began competing in martial arts tournaments and became a five-time undefeated World Middleweight Karate Champion.

Norris made his acting debut in 1972 with a small role in the film "The Way of the Dragon," which was directed by Bruce Lee. He went on to appear in a number of other films, including "Good Guys Wear Black," "A Force of One," and "The Octagon." He also appeared in a number of television series, including "Walker, Texas Ranger," which aired from 1993 to 2001 and became one of his most popular and enduring roles.

In addition to his acting and martial arts careers, Norris is also an author, having written several books on topics such as self-defense and fitness. He is also known for his philanthropic efforts and has supported various charities and causes throughout his career. Norris has been married twice and has five children. He currently resides in Texas.
